Leadership Philosophy

Personal Definition

Leadership is a method that can be taught to and/or learned from experience and in small increments. Leadership guides a person to help others get from where they are to where they would like to be and through all hardships as best as they can.

My Philosophy

Leadership to me is the ability to help anyone out in need. Whether it is something small like giving directions to a location or big like medical help. I believe leadership is something that people are born with as a “natural born leader,” yet to be a leader, some traits or characteristics not everyone is born with. With that being said, I believe leadership can be taught. Being assertive takes some work if a person is a bit shy, but is the leader of a group not by choice. Being a leader takes time, but can be a rewarding experience.

Three Theories/Practices

My definition reflects the Path-Goal Theory, Challenge the Process Practice, and the Trait and Behavioral Approach. I selected these practices/theories because in Path-Goal Theory there is something called “end goal” and that is where everyone would like to be as stated in my definition. Challenge the Process because in commitment 6 it explains that generating small wins and learning from experiences creates an active leader. Trait and Behavioral Approaches were brought up in my definition because leadership can be taught and/learned based on the person’s personality.
